## Key Ideas

Despite its value, deep work is becoming scarce in most organizations. Newport identifies three forces driving this:

- **Open offices** — Designed for serendipitous collaboration, they create constant low-grade distraction.
- **Real-time communication culture** — The expectation of immediate availability (Slack, email) makes sustained focus socially penalized.
- **The metric black hole** — Knowledge work lacks clear productivity metrics, so people default to *busyness* as a proxy for value.

The result: organizations collectively undervalue depth and drift toward shallow, reactive work.

## My Notes

The "metric black hole" concept is particularly relevant in PM work. When it's hard to measure the value of thinking clearly, writing crisply, or planning carefully, visibility replaces output as the performance signal. This is a systems problem, not a personal failing.
